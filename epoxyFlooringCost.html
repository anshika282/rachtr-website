What is Epoxy Flooring?

Epoxy Flooring is a type of synthetic resin floor system, in which especially formulated epoxy coatings are laid on top of concrete substrates as a form of protection and decoration layer.

It transforms a plain concrete slab into a smooth, finished surface that can have customized properties, as per requirement, like -

  • Chemical Resistance, including Acid-Resistant or Solvent Resistance
  • Oil resistance
  • Moisture Resistance
  • Anti-Skid or Non-Slip Property
  • Anti-Static Property
  • Electro static Discharge (ESD) Flooring

FACTORS AFFECTING THE INDUSTRIAL

EPOXY FLOORING COST

When planning industrial epoxy flooring, understanding cost factors is essential. At RachTR, we offer solutions tailored to industries like automobile, pharmaceutical, chemical, and electronics.

Here’s what influences the cost of industrial epoxy flooring, including considerations like the epoxy flooring price per sq ft:

01
Industry Type

Different industries have specific needs.

Pharmaceutical industries require anti-microbial, seamless, and easy-to-clean surfaces to maintain hygiene standards.
02
Specific Requirements

Additional features like ESD control or chemical resistance can increase costs.

Electronics manufacturing floors need anti skid properties.
03
Quantity & Floor Size

Larger areas often get bulk discounts, reducing epoxy flooring costs per unit.

Large warehouses get better rates compared to smaller factories.
04
Material/Chemistry

Material choice significantly affects costs. Epoxy flooring is cost-effective; polyurethane (PU Flooring) options add durability, but costs more.

Epoxy flooring is affordable, while PU flooring is ideal for heavy-duty areas..
05
Thickness Needed

Thicker floors enhance durability but raise costs.

Heavy machinery areas may need thicker epoxy flooring to withstand impact, adding to the expense.
06
Surface Preparation

The condition of the existing surface impacts prep costs.

Fresh RCC surfaces require minimal prep, unlike older surfaces needing leveling.
07
Surface Undulations

Uneven floors require leveling, increasing costs.

Electronics manufacturing floors with undulations need extra prep for stability.
